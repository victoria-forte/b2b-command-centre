1

Pre-Qualified

Initial lead qualified for fit, authority, and B2B relevance

Criteria to Meet

  • Company fit confirmed — industry, revenue size, and B2B use case align with ICP
  • Contact is a decision-maker or a champion has been identified
  • Basic need for B2B wholesale/trade channel validated
  • Lead source and attribution documented in Salesforce
  • Account research completed (website, LinkedIn, existing Shopify presence)
  • Opportunity created in Salesforce with correct territory and segment

Key Questions to Answer

  • Is this a net-new merchant or an existing Shopify merchant expanding to B2B?
  • What is their current B2B platform or process (manual, competitor, none)?
  • What is the estimated annual B2B GMV?
  • Does the merchant sell DTC today, or is this B2B-first?
  • Is there a compelling event or timeline driving urgency?
Move to Envision when: You've confirmed the merchant fits the ICP, a qualified contact is engaged, and you have a discovery call scheduled or completed.
2

Envision

Discovery complete — merchant's vision, goals, and pain points understood

Criteria to Meet

  • Discovery call completed and notes documented in Salesforce
  • Merchant goals and pain points clearly articulated
  • Champion identified and actively engaged
  • Decision-making process mapped — who decides, who influences, approval steps
  • Timeline and key milestones understood (go-live target, budget cycle)
  • Current tech stack and integrations catalogued (ERP, payments, fulfillment)
  • Competitive landscape understood — are they evaluating alternatives?

Key Questions to Answer

  • What does their ideal B2B buying experience look like for their customers?
  • Who are the economic buyer, technical buyer, and end users?
  • What is the approval and procurement process? How long does it take?
  • What is driving the need for change now? What happens if they do nothing?
  • What does success look like in 6-12 months post-launch?
  • Are they evaluating other platforms? What's their evaluation criteria?

See the full Discovery Question Bank for deeper qualification.

Move to Solution when: You fully understand the merchant's goals, pain points, buying process, and technical requirements — and you can articulate why Shopify B2B is the right fit.
3

Solution

Solution designed and validated against merchant requirements

Criteria to Meet

  • Solution Engineer (SE) engaged and attached to the opportunity
  • Technical assessment and scoping completed
  • Solution architecture mapped — blended vs dedicated store, B2B features needed
  • Integration requirements identified (ERP, payments, fulfillment, PIM)
  • Partner identified via Crossbeam and attached if implementation support needed
  • Business case and ROI articulated — value of migrating/adopting Shopify B2B
  • Any product gaps or workarounds documented and communicated to merchant

Key Questions to Answer

  • Can Shopify B2B meet all critical technical requirements?
  • What integrations are required and are they supported (native or via partner)?
  • Is a partner needed for implementation? Which partner is the best fit?
  • Blended or dedicated store — what's the right architecture for this merchant?
  • What is the estimated implementation timeline and complexity?
  • Are there any product gaps? Can they be solved with APIs, apps, or workarounds?

Reference: Working with your SE for SE collaboration best practices.

Move to Demonstrate when: The solution is designed, the SE has validated feasibility, and you're ready to show the merchant a tailored demo that maps to their specific requirements.
4

Demonstrate

Tailored demo delivered — merchant has seen proof of value

Criteria to Meet

  • Customized demo delivered — tailored to merchant's use case, not a generic walkthrough
  • Key stakeholders attended the demo (champion + economic buyer or delegate)
  • Objections surfaced, documented, and addressed or plan to resolve
  • Merchant confirmed Shopify B2B can meet their core needs
  • Relevant case studies and social proof shared
  • Follow-up action items documented with clear owners and deadlines
  • Digital Sales Room or leave-behind materials shared

Key Questions to Answer

  • Did the economic buyer attend or receive a detailed readout?
  • What objections remain unresolved? What is the plan to address them?
  • Has the merchant seen case studies relevant to their industry/use case?
  • Is there consensus among stakeholders that Shopify is the right choice?
  • What are the next steps the merchant has committed to?
  • Are there additional stakeholders who need to be brought into the evaluation?

Reference: B2B Objections Cheat Sheet for handling common objections.

Move to Deal Craft when: The merchant has seen a tailored demo, key stakeholders are aligned, major objections are resolved, and the merchant is ready to discuss commercial terms.
5

Deal Craft

Commercial terms negotiated and contract in progress

Criteria to Meet

  • Pricing configured in CPQ and aligned with B2B opportunity valuation
  • Deal Desk submission completed (if non-standard pricing or terms)
  • MVF (Merchant Value Framework) created and values aligned
  • Contract routed via Ironclad in Salesforce
  • Trial store set up for the merchant
  • Mutual close plan documented with milestones and deadlines
  • Manager involved in deal review and coaching
  • Go-live date agreed and realistic based on implementation scope

Key Questions to Answer

  • Is pricing aligned with the B2B opportunity valuation model?
  • Are payment terms agreed (B2B Payments adoption, DTC Payments, Retail)?
  • Does the go-live date align with the MVF and implementation timeline?
  • Are there any legal or procurement blockers on the merchant side?
  • Has the merchant committed to a credit card on file for the trial store?
  • Is the partner (if applicable) aligned on implementation timeline and scope?
  • Has manager reviewed and approved the deal structure?
Move to Closed when: The contract is signed, MVF is finalized, all Closed Won Checklist items are complete, and the merchant is ready for onboarding and launch.
6

Closed

Deal closed-won — all checklist items complete, merchant moving to onboarding

Criteria to Meet

  • Revenue: Correct DTC, B2B, and Retail revenue aligned with MVF
  • Products: DTC Standard added, B2B Sales Price = $0, POS locations (if applicable), payment products match MVF percentages
  • Launch: SE attached, partner attached, technical assessment uploaded, go-live date aligned with MVF
  • Contacts: All engaged contacts uploaded, launch contacts for B2B and DTC identified
  • Contracts: Trial store set up, credit card on file, MVF completed (incl. B2B and DTC Payments), contract signed
  • Notes: Digital Sales Room link and discovery call notes in Salesforce
  • Files: Mutual plan with milestones, email proof of intent, MVF downloaded and attached, proof of manager involvement

Key Questions to Answer

  • Have all 7 categories of the Closed Won Checklist been completed?
  • Is the SE ready to begin onboarding and implementation support?
  • Is the partner aligned and ready to start implementation?
  • Has a clean handoff to CSM been documented with full context?
  • Are monthly pre-launch check-ins scheduled?
  • Is the launch target (0.5% committed GMV realized) realistic?

Use the full interactive Closed Won Checklist to track every item.

Post-Close: Own the merchant relationship through launch. Run monthly check-ins, collaborate with SE to remove blockers, and ensure a clean handoff to CSM with documented context.
